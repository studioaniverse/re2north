Return to the North: The Virtual Archaeology of Electronic Orbits

Return to the North: Virtual Archaeology of Electronic Orbits is a 6DoF XR interactive narrative work. The historical development of railway networks profoundly reshaped the visual culture and lived experience of cities in northern China. However, this infrastructure paradoxically contributed to the gradual depletion of resources across the region while simultaneously connecting towns.

The Railway orbits not only physically isolated communities but also fragmented them within virtual algorithmic databases. With the rise of large-scale AI models, cultural images are increasingly filtered, often correlating positively with economic development and capital flows. In this sense, digital infrastructure can be seen as parasitizing the remnants of industrial infrastructure. This artwork envisions an optimistic future archaeological landscape where electronic orbits connect data islands, transforming them into distributed virtual narrative spaces.

Railway travel was instrumental in inspiring the transformation of the perception of modernity during the 19th century. Panoramic landscapes and topological space-time redefined movement experiences; the enclosed train carriage became a quintessential metaphor for immersive environments. Nevertheless, contemporary virtual reality narratives, typically designed as synchronous co-presence collaborative environments, may obscure the complexities embedded in the Cyber-Railway Economic Geography — specifically, the fragmented collective memories encoded within cultural landscapes.

This VR artwork poetically constructs an optimistic vision of future archaeology, wherein virtual electronic Orbits bridge these data islands to form a distributed narrative space. The railway serves as the central thread, guiding users through a spatial montage that enables interactive exploration of fragmented urban histories. We collected thousands of images from public media, historical prints and personal archives of four cities in northern China — all of which have been profoundly influenced by railways in their visual culture and feature many monumental landscapes. We used 3D Gaussian and photogrammetry to create an immersive environment, including city squares, amusement parks, and site such as the People's Commune.
